## Why This Stayed With Me

This source reframed preservation for me. It argued that keeping information alive is less about storage and more about making sure future readers can still understand what they are looking at.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Preservation fails when context goes missing

Files can survive while meaning disappears. Naming, dependencies, and surrounding explanations often decay long before the file itself does.

### Archives are active systems

Preservation is not passive. Formats need migration, links need repair, and descriptive layers need updating if an artifact is going to stay legible over time.

### Permanence depends on stewardship

Durability comes from repeated care. What lasts usually has someone tending to it, even if that labor stays invisible.

## What It Changed In My Work

- I value metadata more than I used to.
- I document decisions with future readers in mind.
- I think about how digital work will age, not just how it launches.
