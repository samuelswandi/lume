## Why This Stayed With Me

This source helped me separate reduction from clarity. It reminded me that less only feels meaningful when what remains is precise, intentional, and well framed.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Minimalism is selective emphasis

Removing elements does not solve weak structure. It only exposes it faster.

### Silence requires framing

A sparse interface works when hierarchy and rhythm are still obvious. Without that, emptiness reads like omission rather than restraint.

### Restraint is a service to attention

Good minimal systems reduce interpretive work. They help people spend attention on meaning instead of on figuring out what the interface is trying to say.

## What It Changed In My Work

- I cut less for the sake of appearance.
- I care more about pacing and hierarchy.
- I use quiet interfaces to direct attention, not to perform taste.
