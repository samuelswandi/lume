## Why It Stayed With Me

I liked this book because it extends the judgment conversation beyond bias. It points to a different failure mode: even when people share the same rules, their decisions can still vary wildly.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Variability is often invisible

Organizations usually notice consistent error faster than inconsistent error, even when the randomness is just as costly.

### Judgment needs hygiene

The book argues for process design that reduces arbitrary variation before people ever get to the final call.

### Skill does not eliminate scatter

Experts can still disagree dramatically if the environment allows too much unstructured discretion.

## What I Took From It

- Measure inconsistency, not just bias.
- Use process to reduce avoidable variation.
- Treat judgment quality as a systems problem.
