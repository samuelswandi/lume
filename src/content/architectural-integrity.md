## Why This Stayed With Me

This source made structure feel visible. It framed architecture less as an internal technical artifact and more as the choreography people experience when they move through a product.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Every interface inherits its structure

People do not experience architecture as diagrams. They experience it through sequence, timing, exits, and the amount of effort a path demands.

### Integrity is measured in reroutes

When conditions shift, robust systems adapt through explicit pathways. Weak systems pile up exceptions and hidden detours until no one can explain the product clearly.

### Clarity compounds across layers

Navigation quality, service design, and content decisions often improve together. When structure is crisp in one layer, it tends to support clarity in the others.

## What It Changed In My Work

- I think harder about transitional states and dead ends.
- I treat information architecture as product behavior, not documentation.
- I look for whether a flow can still stay legible when assumptions break.
