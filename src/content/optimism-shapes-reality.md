## Why It Stayed With Me

I like this piece because it compresses a startup truth into a very short argument: the timeline people believe in changes the timeline they produce.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Scope influences duration

When a team believes something will take forever, it behaves differently than when it believes the work can be compressed.

### Optimism is operational

This is not optimism as vibes. It is optimism as a practical force that changes urgency, decision speed, and how much friction a team tolerates.

### Expectations steer execution

The article is memorable because it turns scheduling from neutral planning into a leadership act.

## What I Took From It

- Timelines shape behavior.
- Optimism can be a real execution tool.
- Scope should be set with awareness of its psychological effect.
