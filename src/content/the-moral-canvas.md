## Why This Stayed With Me

This source made interface decisions feel more consequential. It pushed the idea that defaults, friction, and wording are not neutral details; they shape what people are likely to do and what they are allowed to understand.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Convenience is not neutral

The easiest path usually reflects the system's priorities. That means convenience can quietly become a form of persuasion.

### Ethics becomes visible through defaults

A product expresses its values most clearly when the user does nothing. Defaults reveal who bears the cost, who gets access, and what outcomes are considered normal.

### Transparency must survive stress

Clarity during the happy path is not enough. Billing changes, error states, and irreversible actions are where honest design is actually tested.

## What It Changed In My Work

- I notice defaults more quickly.
- I question convenience when it clearly serves the system over the person.
- I treat failure states as part of the ethical surface of a product.
