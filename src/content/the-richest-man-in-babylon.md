## Why It Stayed With Me

This one is simple, but it stays useful because the lessons are stubbornly durable. It is basically a finance discipline book disguised as parables.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Pay yourself first

The most famous lesson works because it forces saving to become structural rather than aspirational.

### Wealth grows through routine

The book treats prosperity less as brilliance and more as repeated behavior under restraint.

### Debt can quietly own the future

Its warnings about obligations still feel current because bad financial decisions usually begin by feeling manageable.

### Opportunity favors the willing

One remembered line from the book: good luck waits to come to the man who accepts opportunities.

## What I Took From It

- Save before spending.
- Respect slow compounding.
- Keep financial discipline boring on purpose.
