## Why This Stayed With Me

This source sharpened the idea that scale is never just about adding more screens or more components. It is about building enough structure that teams can keep making coherent decisions without asking permission every time.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### System primitives precede aesthetics

Before a product feels polished, it needs dependable mechanics. Buttons, inputs, spacing, and hierarchy build trust long before visual flourish does.

### Scale is an editorial problem

Strong systems are curated. They grow through decisions about what deserves to remain, what needs to be merged, and what should never have shipped in the first place.

### Governance shows up in the seams

The real test is what happens when new flows are assembled under pressure. If the transitions still feel intentional, the system is carrying its weight.

## What It Changed In My Work

- I pay more attention to naming and taxonomy.
- I treat component libraries as living systems, not storage bins.
- I look for the seams where inconsistency first appears.
