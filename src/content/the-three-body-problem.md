## Why It Stayed With Me

This book keeps widening the frame until everyday human assumptions start to feel temporary. I like it because it mixes scientific imagination with a colder civilizational perspective than most fiction attempts.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Scale changes morality

When the frame expands to civilizations and survival horizons, familiar ethical instincts stop feeling stable.

### Science can become existential

The novel makes research feel like more than discovery. It becomes political, historical, and strategic all at once.

### Contact is not automatically uplifting

The book is memorable partly because it refuses the comforting version of first contact.

## What I Took From It

- Bigger frames produce different fears.
- Scientific progress is never socially neutral.
- Some stories are useful because they unsettle the default human scale.
