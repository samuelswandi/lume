## Why It Stayed With Me

Sam Altman's essays feel less like polished thought leadership and more like an operating manual for ambitious people. I keep coming back to them because the advice is usually direct, cumulative, and easy to test against real work.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Agency compounds

Many of the essays reward the same trait: taking initiative early, asking for responsibility, and acting before permission becomes a bottleneck.

### Long-term leverage matters

The writing repeatedly points toward choices that create option value over time instead of short-term prestige alone.

### Important waves arrive unevenly

The blog often frames technology shifts as windows that feel obvious only after they have already started.

## What I Took From It

- Pick environments where initiative is rewarded.
- Optimize for compounding opportunities.
- Stay close to fields that are still being rewritten.
