## Why It Stayed With Me

This is one of the books I revisit when I want to widen the frame. Charlie Munger's value is not one insight but the insistence that good judgment usually comes from many disciplines working together.

## Notes I Kept Returning To

### Mental models are practical, not decorative

The point is not to collect frameworks. The point is to have enough lenses to avoid being trapped by one.

### Incentives explain more than people admit

Munger returns to incentives constantly because systems often behave exactly as they are rewarded to behave.

### Worldly wisdom is cumulative

Better thinking comes from stacking small durable ideas rather than waiting for a single master theory.

## What I Took From It

- Expand the range of models you can use.
- Check incentives before trusting stated intentions.
- Avoid single-discipline explanations for complex problems.
